The Diderot Effect states that obtaining a new possession often creates a spiral of consumption that leads to additional purchases.
You can spot this pattern everywhere. You buy a dress and have to get new shoes and earrings to match. You buy a couch and suddenly question the layout of your entire living room. You buy a toy for your child and soon find yourself purchasing all of the accessories that go with it. You buy a digital camera and the next thing a fixed lens replaced the kit lens

It’s a chain reaction of purchases. You can use the connectedness of behavior to your advantage.
